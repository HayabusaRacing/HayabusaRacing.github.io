Summary of the Simulation

We used a combination of Newton's Second Law of motion and the quadratic drag force to model the velocity of the car as a function of time. The model only accounts for two forces: Constant thrust from the CO2 cartridge and quadratic air drag. \[F_{drag} = \frac{1}{2} \rho C_d A v^2 = k v^2\] \[F_{total} = m a = F_{thrust} - F_{drag}\] \[a = \frac{F_{total}}{m}\] \[\frac{dv}{dt} = \frac{F_{thrust} - kv^2}{m}\] This separable differential equation could be solved analytically, and turns out to form a tangent hyperbolic equation. On this page, we used the 4th-order Runge-Kutta method to numerically solve the differential equation and plot the velocity versus time graph.

We assumed that the affecting factors of the car's lap times are listed below:

  • Mass of the car
  • Aerodynamic drag
  • Aerodynamic lift
  • Thrust from the CO2 cartridge
  • Bearing friction
  • Rolling resistance
  • Wheel inertia
  • Cartridge-caused tipping moment
  • Aerodynamically-caused tipping moment
The following following forces will be scribed as a function of time: \[F_{thrust}(t) \text{: the force created by the cartridge thrust}\] \[F_{drag}(t) \text{: the force created by aerodynamic drag}\] \[F_{lift}(t) \text{: the force created by aerodynamic lift}\] \[F_{friction}(t) \text{: the force created by bearing friction}\] \[F_{rolling}(t) \text{: the force created by rolling resistance}\] In addition, the mass is expected to alter with respect to time, as the cartridge is used up, thus, we will scribe the mass as a function of time: \[m(t) \text{: the mass of the car at time } t\]
